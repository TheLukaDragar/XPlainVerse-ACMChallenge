# --- 1. Primary VLM user prompt --------------------------------------------
# Used for 50% of training rows in v2 (down from 67% in v1) AND for VLM-only
# fallback inference when Pass-1 is unavailable or low-confidence.
=== VLM_USER_PROMPT ===
TASK: Decide whether this image is a real photograph or AI-generated.
Examine the style and the synthesis artifacts. Identify several specific
objects or regions in the image (typically 4–6) and, in one coherent
paragraph, describe the visual evidence visible in each — distorted text,
warped geometry, smudged or merged textures, overly soft or plastic
skin and fabric, regions that lack fine detail or blend into surrounding
textures, anatomical errors, inconsistent lighting or shadows, unnatural
object boundaries, or, for real images, authentic everyday cues such as
wrinkles, crinkles, motion blur, dirt, messy textures, candid expressions,
and lots of small natural details that would be hard to invent. Use
connectives such as "Most notably" and "Additionally" to chain the
observations together.

End your response with a new line containing exactly:
Verdict: real
or
Verdict: fake
=== END ===


# --- 2. Hypothetical-fake prompt (Pass-2 inference template) ---------------
# Used for 25% of training rows in v2 AND for Pass-2 inference when Pass-1
# has determined the image to be FAKE.
# The preamble matches the 27-May gold-verdict validation experiment.
# Body vocabulary mined from 2000 fake GTs (research/07_gt_vocabulary_analysis.md):
# verb "appears" (1.6/sample), qualifier "overly", negation "lacks/lacking",
# phrase "blends into surrounding", body intensifier "particularly", and the
# canonical "Most notably ... Additionally ..." transition template (270+ of
# 2000 fakes use it verbatim).
=== VLM_USER_PROMPT_HYPOTHETICAL_FAKE ===
FORENSIC ANALYSIS — a separate AI-generated-content detector has determined
this image is FAKE. Provide the forensic explanation supporting that
determination.

Identify several specific objects or regions in the image (typically 4–6)
and, in one coherent paragraph, describe how each region appears
unnatural — for example, overly soft or plastic textures, regions that
lack fine detail, edges that blend into surrounding objects without
proper boundaries, distorted text, warped geometry, smudged or merged
textures, anatomical errors, or inconsistent lighting or shadows. Start
the paragraph with "Most notably" to introduce the strongest piece of
evidence, then use "Additionally" to chain the remaining observations.
Use "particularly" inside sentences to point to specific subregions.

End your response with a new line containing exactly:
Verdict: fake
=== END ===


# --- 3. Hypothetical-real prompt (Pass-2 inference template) ---------------
# Used for 25% of training rows in v2 AND for Pass-2 inference when Pass-1
# has determined the image to be REAL.
# Soft-match style (per user, 27-May): keeps the forensic framing but adds
# the plain-English authentic-cue vocabulary mined from 2000 real GTs:
# wrinkles/crinkles, messy/dirty surfaces, candid expressions, motion blur,
# lots of small natural details. Real GTs are 41-word median, written in
# everyday English — full forensic prose would be a register mismatch.
=== VLM_USER_PROMPT_HYPOTHETICAL_REAL ===
FORENSIC ANALYSIS — a separate AI-generated-content detector has determined
this image is REAL. Provide the forensic explanation supporting that
determination.

In plain everyday language, identify several specific things in the
image that make it look like a real photograph — for example wrinkles
or crinkles in clothing, messy or dirty surfaces, hair or fabric moving
naturally, candid or unforced facial expressions, motion blur, natural
lighting and shadows, or lots of small natural details that would be
hard to invent. Aim for one short paragraph (roughly 1–3 sentences).

End your response with a new line containing exactly:
Verdict: real
=== END ===
